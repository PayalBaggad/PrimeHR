This page describes how Techstuff Pvt Ltd (operating PrimeHR) approaches compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"). This applies to customers and users in the European Union (EU) and European Economic Area (EEA).

1 Overview

The GDPR is a comprehensive data protection law that governs how organisations collect, use, and store personal data of individuals in the EU/EEA. We are committed to processing personal data responsibly and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

If you are a resident of the EU or EEA, you have specific rights regarding your personal data that are described in this document.

2 Our Role Under GDPR

Data Controller

When we collect your personal data directly (e.g. when you visit our website, sign up for a demo, or contact us), we act as a Data Controller — meaning we determine the purposes and means of processing.

Data Processor

When your employer (our customer) uses PrimeHR to manage your HR data, we act as a Data Processor — meaning we process data on behalf of and under the instructions of your employer. Your employer is the Data Controller in this scenario.

We enter into a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with all customers who require one for GDPR compliance.

3 Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under the following legal bases as defined in Article 6 GDPR:

  • Contract: Processing necessary to perform our contract with you or your employer
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing for our legitimate business interests (e.g. fraud prevention, platform security) where not overridden by your rights
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required by applicable law (e.g. tax and employment law)
  • Consent: Where you have given explicit consent (e.g. marketing communications)

4 Data We Process

As a Data Processor on behalf of your employer, we may process the following categories of data:

  • Identity data — name, employee code, designation
  • Contact data — work email address
  • Employment data — salary, attendance, leave, payslips
  • Location data — GPS coordinates at time of attendance check-in/out
  • Device data — device type, OS, used for attendance logging

Full details of data processed are described in our Privacy Policy.

5 Your Rights

Under the GDPR, EU/EEA residents have the following rights:

Right of Access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (Article 15)

Right to Rectification

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data (Article 16)

Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances (Article 17)

Right to Restriction

Request that we restrict processing of your data (Article 18)

Right to Portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (Article 20)

Right to Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests (Article 21)

Since your employer is the Data Controller for employment-related data, many requests should be directed to your HR department in the first instance. We will assist our customers in fulfilling your rights requests within the required timeframes.

6 Data Transfers

PrimeHR is operated from India. If you are located in the EU/EEA, your personal data may be transferred to and processed in India, which is outside the European Economic Area.

We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as approved by the European Commission
  • Data Processing Agreements with our sub-processors that include appropriate transfer mechanisms

7 Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. Retention periods are determined by:

  • Your employer's HR data retention policies
  • Applicable legal and regulatory requirements
  • Our legitimate business needs (e.g. dispute resolution, audit trails)

Upon contract termination, customer data is made available for export for 30 days, after which it is securely deleted.

8 Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS/HTTPS
  • Secure storage of authentication tokens using platform-native secure storage
  • Access controls and role-based permissions
  • Regular security reviews and vulnerability assessments

9 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will:

  • Notify our affected customers (Data Controllers) without undue delay and within 72 hours of becoming aware
  • Provide all necessary information to assist customers in notifying the relevant supervisory authority and affected individuals
  • Document all breaches in our internal breach register
